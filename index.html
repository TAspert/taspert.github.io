Curriculum vitae

Hi,

My name is Théo ASPERT. I am a french engineer and scientist passionated about the biology of aging. I hold a PhD in biophysics and an engineering degree in instrumental physics for biology.

I did my PhD in the lab of Gilles Charvin where I developed several tools based on microfluidics, timelapse microscopy, image analysis and deep-learning (available in Resources) to address fascinating questions about replicative aging and quiescence in budding yeast, from a quantitative and single-cell prospect

I also worked on different side projects in collaboration with other labs, for which I mainly developed microfluidic tools (see Research and Publications).

Reproducibility, and therefore, clear protocols, conditions and instructions, are a key point of today's and tomorrow's science. Hence, I also created this website in part to detail some of the tools I developed and used.

I have a strong methodological background, especially in microfluidics, but the question that haunts me is: "Why and how do we age?!". Therefore, I am looking for a post-doctoral or research engineer (or equivalent) position where I could put my expertise to work and help understand aging!
