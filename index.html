Curriculum vitae

My name is Théo ASPERT, I am a french engineer and scientist passionate about the biology of aging. I hold a PhD in biophysics, a master's degree in bioengineering (specialized in physics and instrumentation for biomedical applications) and a master of science (specialized in nanobiology).

I did my PhD in the lab of Gilles Charvin, in IGBMC, where I developed several tools based on microfluidics, long-term single-cell microscopy, image analysis and deep-learning (available in Resources) to address fascinating questions about replicative aging and quiescence, from a quantitative and single-cell prospect.

I have also worked on different side projects in collaboration with other labs (see Research and Publications).

Reproducibility, and therefore, clear protocols, conditions and instructions, are a critical aspect of today's and tomorrow's science. Hence, I also created this website to describe in details the tools I developed and used.

Beyond developing tools, the haunting question to me is: "How do most of living systems age, and how is their progeny rejuvenated?!". Therefore, I am looking for a post-doctoral position where I could put my expertise to work and help address this question!
